Dr. Ming Wu is focused on two central scientific problems within the precision manufacturing of complex components: multi-scale process consistency and multi-condition adaptability. This work is pursued through long-term investigations on smart manufacturing that are driven by both physical principles and data-driven methodologies. 
A proposed hybrid jet electrochemical machining process that employs photolithography masks enables the consistent mass production of multi-scale microstructures on difficult-to-machine materials. A theoretical framework was developed, integrating a hybrid-scale mesh with a virtual gap methodology, which permits high-efficiency, multi-physics simulations at sub-micrometer resolution. This work uncovers non-linear coupling mechanisms. An intelligent quality control framework based on limited-label, multi-source sensing was also constructed, reducing annotation costs while improving model accuracy and generalisation.
With over 50 published papers, the research has received recognition from international academicians, CIRP fellows, and journal editors, and was featured by KU Leuven. As a significant contributor to European research projects (€16.55 million), Dr. Wu’s work established an innovation chain from theory to industrial translation. The research has been validated by eight European companies, including ASML supplier VDL, and generated approximately €68,000 in initial service revenue.
